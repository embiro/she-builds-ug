Step 1 — Getting Applications

The Application System

📋 What the Form Collects

  • 1. Full name of business owner
  • 2. Business name
  • 3. Type of business (dropdown)
  • 4. District / location in Uganda
  • 5. Phone number (WhatsApp preferred)
  • 6. What digital solution do you need? (website / app)
  • 7. Describe your business in 2–3 sentences
  • 8. Do you currently have a website or app? (Yes/No)
  • 9. How did you hear about this campaign?

⚙️ How It's Managed

  • → Google Form auto-populates to a Google Sheet
  • → Queen checks new submissions twice daily (9 AM + 4 PM)
  • → Each applicant gets auto-acknowledgement within 2 hours
  • → Sheet has columns: Name / Business / Status / Assigned Dev / Notes
  • → Status options: Applied → Reviewing → Selected → Rejected → Waitlisted → Building → Live
  • → Elly reviews all submissions daily and marks top picks
  • → Final selection approved by Collins

Tool: Google Forms + Google Sheets. Free. No setup cost.

Update the tracker: DMs sent, responses received, new applications, group visited. Takes 5 minutes. Non-negotiable — this is how we measure progress.

Step 4 — Building & Delivering

How We Build and Deliver

📞

Day 1: Onboarding Call

Developer calls the client. 30 minutes. Confirms: business name, colours, content, pages needed. Client gets 5 days to send all content.


Owner: Assigned Dev
⚙️

Days 2–7: Build

Developer builds using standard Embiro template. Fahad reviews at 50% and 100%. Client gets 2 rounds of minor revisions only.


Owner: Dev + Fahad
🚀

Day 8: Launch

Site goes live. Client receives login details, how-to guide, and 10-day support number. Embiro posts a launch celebration on social media — free PR for the client.


Owner: Fahad + Elly

⚠️ Rules That Protect the Team

  • → No build starts without all content received
  • → Max 5 projects per developer in parallel
  • → Only 2 revision rounds — scope creep is out
  • → Client misses 5-day content deadline → moves to next batch
  • → All sites on shared hosting account (Embiro manages)

📊 Project Tracking Board

Use a shared Google Sheet or Trello board with columns:

📋 Onboarding ⏳ Waiting for Content ⚙️ Building 👀 In Review ✅ Live ❌ Stalled

Fahad updates this daily. Collins can see it at any time.

Risk Management

What Could Go Wrong & What We Do

Risk Likelihood What We Do
Too few applications (less than 100) Medium Activate secondary networks faster. Ask NextMedia for an extra push. Add a WhatsApp broadcast to all Embiro contacts.
Too many low-quality applications High Tighten screening criteria. Prioritise upcountry, active businesses, and group nominations over random signups.
Clients don't send content in time High Strict 5-day rule. If missed, they move to waitlist and next applicant is pulled. No exceptions.
Developer capacity overload Medium Cap at 5 active projects per dev. Stagger selection announcements. Only select 25/week, not all 100 at once. Builds roll as applications are approved.
NextMedia doesn't push hard enough Low Double down on group visits and organic social. This is why we run 3 channels simultaneously.
Team burns out by Week 3 Medium Daily routine is designed to be 2–3 hours max. Elly owns outreach. Queen owns admin. Fahad owns builds. Don't cross streams.
